Work Experience

Shadow Health 2012-2013

I helped develop a Ruby on Rails web app for Nursing Education, which integrated with a 3D virtual patient implemented in Unity3D and took the product from an opinionated app with two Course types and fixed content, to a dynamic system where instructional content and functionality are separated. When I started, instructional content was contained in hashes in the codebase, with copyedits from nurses going through development and the usual version control and deployment workflow; creating a new course type required a lot of development work, and close integration between development and content teams.

In order to expand the app, I helped design and implement a system where courses acted as containers. The current instructional features of the app were moved into various models sharing a common interface, allowing content creators to work independently of development. To allow for archiving and the exchange of course content between instances, I implemented a JSON export library which I was able to detach as a Gem.

Additionally, I laid a development roadmap for implementing version control of content, and for separating creation and consumption of content into separate apps. Currently, some content is already separated and versioned in such a system. App response time also improved and became more consistent in this time period, even though our use of caching is almost nonexistent compared to the earlier versions of the app.

Infotech Inc. 2011-2012

The service acts as an easy-to-use wrapper around the GPG encryption system, allowing secure encrypted bidding on DOT projects (used or mandated by most DOTs in the US). I researched common issues and authored a Training Guide targeting our major platforms (Windows XP, Vista, 7).

In particular, I researched an issue with the PGP key creation GUI and proposed a solution to the development team which was implemented largely from my design. Additionally, I streamlined weekly pre-release testing by using Ruby and ERB to automatically generate configuration files and projects, essentially allowing us to build a testing environment with one command. In support of a major version release, I travelled personally to several FDOT offices to train and assist with opening of bids in the revised system. In the process of transitioning DOTs to the new system and on-boarding new agencies, I also converted forms into a proprietary configuration format and debugged issues encountered in test-runs.
